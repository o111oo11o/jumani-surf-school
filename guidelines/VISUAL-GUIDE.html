01 ‚Äî Foundation

Design Principles

The Jumani website should feel like the experience itself: warm, inviting, sun-soaked, and personal. Every visual decision should reflect a small, authentic surf school ‚Äî not a corporate resort brand.

01

Mobile First, Always

Most visitors discover Jumani on their phone while traveling. Design every component for a 375px viewport first, then scale up. The booking CTA must always be one tap away.

02

Warm & Personal

Avoid cold, corporate design patterns. Use rounded corners, organic shapes, handwritten accents, and warm tones. The site should feel like a friendly postcard, not a hotel chain.

03

Fresh & Tropical

The teal-to-yellow gradient palette evokes ocean-to-sunset energy. Colors should feel vibrant and alive, like tropical fruit and turquoise water ‚Äî never dark or heavy.

04

Effortless Clarity

Visitors should understand what Jumani offers within 3 seconds. Three services ‚Äî surf lessons, board rentals, smoothie bar ‚Äî must be visible immediately, with clear CTAs.

Testimonial Style

Quote cards use the handwritten font for the quotation mark, the body font for the quote text, and an italic attribution line. Consider a subtle teal left border or a torn-paper edge effect inspired by the visual references.

Section Eyebrow Pattern

Every content section starts with a small uppercase eyebrow label above the heading. This creates visual rhythm and helps scanners orient quickly. Format: all caps, 11‚Äì12px, bold weight, teal color, with an optional short line before it.

Mix surf action (lessons, waves, boards) with lifestyle (bar, drinks, relaxing, community). Show real people.

Intimate Scale

Photos should feel personal and close ‚Äî small groups, individual moments, close-up textures. Not wide resort panoramas.

Layered Composition

Inspired by the visual references: overlap images, use torn paper edges, tilted frames, and collage-style gallery layouts.

Hero Images

Hero sections use full-width images with a dark gradient overlay (from bottom) for text legibility. The overlay fades from transparent at top to 60‚Äì70% dark at bottom where the heading sits. Keep the focus of the photo in the upper 60% of the frame to avoid text overlap with the main subject.

Gallery Layout

Use a masonry or asymmetric grid for gallery sections ‚Äî inspired by the collage aesthetic in the visual references. Mix image sizes (some full-width, some half-width, occasional polaroid-style with a white border and slight rotation).

07 ‚Äî Mood

Visual References & Mood

Key patterns extracted from the provided visual references. These inform the overall feel and creative direction.

Reference Takeaways

A

Torn Paper & Texture

Ref 1 (camping page) uses torn paper edge dividers and textured backgrounds. Apply this as optional decorative dividers between sections, but keep it subtle ‚Äî a nod, not a theme.

B

Playful Typography Mix

Refs 1‚Äì3 use bold display type alongside handwritten/script fonts. Adopt this dual-font approach: Playfair Display for structure, Caveat for warmth.

C

Soft, Rounded Mobile Cards

Refs 2‚Äì3 (animal farm, tour agency) use rounded cards with green/natural tones on mobile-width layouts. These directly inform our service card and nav patterns.

D

Warm, Nature-Forward Palette

Ref 4 (wellness retreat) demonstrates green/sage/cream used elegantly with layered photos and serif type. This is the sophistication target for Jumani's premium feel.

Design sweet spot: The Jumani website lives between the playful collage energy of references 1‚Äì3 and the refined elegance of reference 4. It should feel crafted and personal (not a template), but also clean and easy to navigate on a phone.

08 ‚Äî Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts

‚úì Do
  • Use the full teal-to-yellow gradient for hero/CTA backgrounds
  • Round all corners generously (16px+ for cards)
  • Use the handwritten font for short, warm accent phrases
  • Show real photos of Mani, Fasil, the beach, and the bar
  • Make "Book Now" the most prominent element on every page
  • Add micro-interactions on button hover (lift + shadow)
  • Use Sand Light (#f8f6f0) as the page background ‚Äî warmer than pure white
  • Overlap elements slightly for depth (images over sections, badges over cards)
‚úó Don't
  • Use sharp/square corners ‚Äî this isn't a corporate or tech brand
  • Use generic stock photography or AI-generated images
  • Use the lighter greens/yellows as text colors (contrast fails)
  • Overload the handwritten font ‚Äî it's an accent, not a workhorse
  • Use a pure white (#fff) page background ‚Äî too clinical
  • Create complex navigation ‚Äî keep it simple and flat
  • Design desktop-first and shrink down ‚Äî start at 375px
  • Use dark/moody color schemes ‚Äî the brand is sun, sea, and smiles
